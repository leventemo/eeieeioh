inspiration

When designing new activity types, we always focus on exploring what interactivity can bring to the table. Think about them as a collection of your favourite worksheets – but not on paper. That means they can be more dynamic, visually and in terms of content, they can give students immediate feedback, they might resemble online games and they can offer direct links to videos, spoken and written text or anything really on the Internet.

The word inspiration has very nice connotations but the idea here is very simple and practical: whenever there is a need for student or teacher to have quick and easy access to any type of prompt in class or at home, we try to make it available online. The truth is, we have much more ideas than we can cope with in terms of development and content writing but we like it this way. Better to have ideas you can't make happen just yet than the other way round.

target audience

Although in order to achieve successful learning, teachers need students, students don't always need teachers. We're trying to make the life of both groups easier and that means the tools and content you find here should be designed to be used in and/or outside of the classroom.

plans

Lots of. We hope to see the site grow and become a full-blown learning platform one day. We are working on our own digital versions of well-tried clasroom activities and games and experimenting with new ideas.
