Activities

play_circle

We're working on a variety of activities; they're going to be the backbone of eeieeioh. Each of them are designed for students to do different things with English. They each require a specific "setup" of how many learners should do them (learners on their own, in pairs, small groups or as a whole class), who has a phone with the prompts and how they are suppesed to use them, is there a need for a projector, etc. When opening an activity, click on theinfo_outline icon for instructions.

Cards

The simplest activity of all: students press a button, get some prompt, then do something. As a teacher, I kind of enjoy not having to photocopy, cut or sort cards into a given number of pairs/groups before class any more. Ever.

Timed Cards

These are Cards, as seen above, but with a timer: teachers sometimes want to restrict or stretch the time they expect their students to work with each single card.

Expandables

Our expandables are not expendable as they are a tool for eliciting ideas. If done as a whole class with a projector in use, it gives the teacher control over the moment the hidden text is revealed. It might work with autonomous learners going over their lists on their phones as well. The activity was designed for error correction, as a prelude to Duels, which is a fast-paced game. We tend to use the two together.

Reword

Aiming at recycling and internalising chunks: fixed phrases, idioms, time expressions, dependent prepositions, etc. Players get 2 points for rephrasing a sentence on their own or 1 point for doing the same after getting a clue, which is a keyword from the target phrase.

Duels

Not unlike Cards but it has timers and counters for 2 players who take turns to quickly chose between a correct and an incorrect form of a word, a phrase or anything really. Designed for quick and somewhat personalized error correction. It works best when the same content is done on Expandables first.

Marksheets

A tool for mock exams that enables teachers to record results and get insights into how individual students and groups are making progress and what needs to be worked on. Coming soon.
